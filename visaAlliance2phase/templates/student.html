Student, Graduate & Training Visas

The Australian Government provides an opportunity to all international students to study in the country but as a prerequisite, you’ll have to obtain a student visa first. The visa you need will depend on your age, the type of study you want to pursue, and how long you want to stay in Australia. As an international student, you would most commonly require a Student visa (subclass 500), regardless of your field of study.

Student visa subclass 500

The Student (Subclass 500) visa lets genuine international students stay in Australia for the duration of their study program, up to a maximum five years.

KEY HIGHLIGHTS:

  • 1) You can be in or outside Australia to lodge a valid application for this visa.
  • 2) If you are under 18 years of age, you will also need to provide additional documents such as parental consent.
  • 3)You must meet health and character requirements.

*Please note that these are the basic requirements, speak with one of our immigration consultants for a detailed list of documents required for visa processing

This visa has two streams namely, Graduate Work Stream and Post Study Work Stream.

  • 1) Proof of enrolment (your electronic confirmation of enrolment)
  • 2) A valid passport
  • 3) Your visa application fee
  • 4) Your Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) statement
  • 5) Academic and work experience documents
  • 6) Evidence of your English proficiency skills (IELTS)

  • 8) Details of your health insurance policy (Overseas Student Health Cover )
  • 9) Statement of purpose
  • 10) Passport sized photographs
2. Applying for your visa online?

You will need to create an account using DIBP’s online application system – ImmiAccount to complete and submit your student visa application. You can also track the status of your visa through ImmiAccount once you have submitted it. You should submit your application no later than six weeks before your course starts, and no earlier than 12 weeks.

Streamlined Processing


Some international students are eligible for streamlined processing depending on the country to which they belong and the college that they have received admission from. These students do not need to provide evidence of proficiency in the English language or evidence of sufficient funds. They only need to provide a declaration that they meet these requirements. Contact us if you are unsure whether you qualify for streamlined processing.


Making sure your visa remains valid


Once you get your visa, there are a number of things you need to do to ensure it remains valid, including:

  • 1) Remain enrolled and maintain satisfactory course progress and attendance
  • 2) Provide your Australian address to your university or college so they can contact you (don’t forget to let them know if you move)
  • 3) Continue to be able to financially support yourself while in Australia
  • 4) Don’t breach the working conditions that apply to your visa

If you want to change your qualification level you will need to apply for a new student visa.

Student Guardian visa subclass 590

The Student Guardian (Subclass 590) visa is for persons who wish to come to Australia to provide care and support for a student visa holder who is under 18 years of age or older due to exceptional circumstances.

Basic eligibility for visas under this category are:

  1. This is a temporary visa. The length of your visa will be determined by the student visa holder’s stay and their age.
  2. You must be a parent, a custodian or a relative. If you are a relative of the student, you must be:
    1. aged 21 years or older,
    2. nominated in writing by a parent or person who has custody of the student.
  3. You can be in or outside Australia to lodge a valid application for this visa.
  4. You can care for more than one student on this visa.
  5. You can study an English Language Intensive Course for Overseas Students (ELICOS) for less than 20 hours per week, or any other study or training for no more than 3 months.
  6. You cannot work.
  7. You can stay until the length of the student visa holder’s stay or when that student turns 18.
  8. You must be a Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE)
  9. You must show evidence of financial ability to support yourself and the student (living expenses, expenses for dependents, tuition fees, return airfare)
  10. You must hold a valid health insurance policy (Overseas Visitor Health Cover)
  11. You must meet health and character requirements.

*Please note that these are the basic requirements, speak with one of our immigration consultants for a detailed list of documents required for visa processing


Secondary Applicants

Subclass 590 visa does not allow secondary applicants unless specific circumstances exist.

  1. Children 6 years old or older can come with you but they must apply for their own student visa.
  2. Children under 6 years old can come only if
    1. there are compelling and compassionate reasons or
    2. granting the visa would significantly benefit the relationship between the Australian Government and the government of another country
  3. Spouses and partners are not allowed as secondary applicants on this visa.

Training visa subclass 407

The Training (Subclass 407) visa, allows you to take part in workplace-based occupational training activities to improve your skills for your job, area of tertiary study, field of expertise or in a professional development training program with a designated sponsor in Australia.

KEY HIGHLIGHTS

  1. You may be in or outside Australia to lodge a valid application for this visa.
  2. This visa allows you to live in Australia to complete a workplace-based training.
  3. This is a Temporary visa and allows you to stay in Australia for up to two years.
  4. You must be at least 18 years of age unless you can demonstrate that exceptional circumstances exist.
  5. You must have a sponsor who is or has applied to be a temporary activities sponsor.
  6. You must be nominated to participate in the sponsor’s nominated program of occupational training.
  7. The nominated program must be offered as a genuine training opportunity.
  8. The nomination must meet one of the three eligibility types:
    1. Occupational training required for registration
    2. Occupational training to enhance skills
    3. Occupational training for capacity building overseas.
  9. You must have at least ‘Functional English’.
  10. You must maintain adequate arrangements for health insurance.
  11. You must have a genuine intention to stay temporarily in Australia and for the purpose of taking part in occupational training activities.
  12. You must be able to demonstrate that you have access to sufficient funds to cover the cost of your stay in Australia.
  13. You must satisfy certain Public Interest criteria and Special Return criteria.
  14. You must meet health and character requirements.
